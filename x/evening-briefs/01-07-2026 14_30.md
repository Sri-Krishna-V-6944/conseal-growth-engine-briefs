## CONTENT IDEAS

### Tweet 1
The question most teams skip before putting documents into an AI agent: "Does the model actually need to see the client's name to answer my question?" Most of the time, it doesn't. Anonymize first, analyze second. Same result, zero exposure.

### Tweet 2
Legaltech has an MCP problem, but it also has a data exposure problem. Building connectors between legal software and AI agents is the easy part. Making sure those connectors don't pipe client PII into a third-party model is the part most people skip.

### Tweet 3
Enterprise AI vendors publish data terms. Those terms can change. The only durable solution is one where sensitive data never leaves your machine in its original form. Process locally, analyze with AI, keep the original behind your firewall.

### Thread 1: Three questions to ask before putting documents into an AI agent
1. Where does the document go? (Your network? Vendor cloud? Both?)
2. What parts of the document does the AI actually need? (All of it? Just the clauses? Only the summary?)
3. What happens to the document after processing? (Deleted? Stored for training? Indexed?)
A thread walking through each question with practical answers. Ends with: if you cannot answer all three, anonymize before you upload.

### Thread 2: The hidden cost of redacting documents manually
Manual redaction takes time, misses things, and doesn't scale to AI workflows. A thread comparing manual redaction vs. automated anonymization for different use cases (legal discovery, medical records, internal knowledge bases). Positions automated local anonymization as the pragmatic middle ground.
